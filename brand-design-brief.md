# trace.memorial — Brand Refresh Brief
## For use in Claude Design

---

## What we are building

Facebook cover image (1640 × 624) and profile image (800 × 800) for the trace.memorial Facebook page.

trace.memorial is a pet bereavement memorial service. Each memorial page is built by a trained counsellor through the TRACE method. The emotional register should be **warm, hopeful, and gentle** — closer to "celebration of a life" than "funeral home."

---

## The problem with the current design

The existing mockups use a dark espresso brown (#2d1f0e) as the dominant background. It reads as too somber and heavy. **Drop the dark brown as a background surface entirely.** It can stay as a text/type colour, but it should not fill large areas.

---

## Brand mark (confirmed)

- **Icon:** a leaf mark — a simple pointed oval (vesica shape) with a single vertical centre line. Outline only, no fill.
- **Wordmark:** `trace.memorial` in a warm serif (Georgia). The `.memorial` portion rendered at ~45% opacity of the `trace` colour.
- These two elements are fixed. Do not change them.

---

## Colour direction

### Primary surface
Warm cream: `#fdfaf0`
Use this as the dominant background on both the cover and profile image.

### Type colour
Dark warm brown for text only: `#2d1f0e`
Used for headlines and the wordmark. Not as a background fill.

### The palette strip — make more of this
The nine memorial page palettes are the heart of the brand identity. They represent the fact that every memorial is personal and individual. Use them more prominently than just a 4px hairline.

**The strip should feel like a meaningful design element, not a footer detail.** Ideas to try:
- A band of colour swatches 16–24px tall, perhaps with soft rounded ends or a subtle fade
- Or a row of small filled leaf marks, one per palette colour, arranged in a line
- Or a soft wash — a gradient that passes through several palette colours at low saturation — used as a full-bleed background tint behind the main content

The strip does not need to include all nine colours if fewer looks better. Priority palette accents, in warmth order:

| Name       | Accent      | Panel bg    |
|------------|-------------|-------------|
| Sunset     | `#c0673b`   | `#fae8dc`   |
| Golden     | `#b8860b`   | `#f7f0d8`   |
| Rose       | `#b5507a`   | `#f7dcea`   |
| Meadow     | `#4a7c59`   | `#e6f0e6`   |
| Sky        | `#3a7bbf`   | `#ddeeff`   |
| Ocean      | `#2a6496`   | `#e0ecf4`   |
| Lavender   | `#7c5c9e`   | `#ede0f7`   |
| Forest     | `#3a5f3a`   | `#e4ece0`   |
| Monochrome | `#2c2c2c`   | `#e8e8e8`   |

For a softer, less rainbow effect, use the **panel bg** colours (the lighter column) rather than the accent colours. They are all pastel-toned and will blend naturally on a cream surface.

## Tone to aim for

- A beautiful letter written about someone loved
- A garden in soft afternoon light
- Premium and restrained, but alive — not cold, not dark
- Accessible to someone in grief: not overwhelming, not clinical

---

## Copy to use on the cover

**Label (small caps, spaced):**
MEMORIAL PAGES FOR THE ANIMALS WE LOVED

**Headline:**
A permanent home for their story.

**Wordmark:**
trace.memorial

---

## What to avoid

- Dark backgrounds of any kind
- The paw-in-circle mark (replaced by the leaf)
- Bright or saturated colours used at full strength — everything should feel like it has been softened by light
- Clutter — the design should feel spacious
